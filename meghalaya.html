WANGALA FESTIVAL:JADOH

Jadoh is a traditional dish enjoyed during the Wangala festival, a harvest festival celebrated by the Garo tribe in Meghalaya. Wangala typically takes place in November and is a joyous occasion to give thanks for a bountiful harvest.Jadoh is a flavorful rice dish cooked with meat, usually pork or chicken, and a blend of aromatic spices. The rice is often infused with turmeric, giving it a vibrant yellow color and a subtle earthy flavor.The meat is marinated with spices such as ginger, garlic, black pepper, and bay leaves before being cooked together with the rice, imparting its rich flavor and tenderness to the dish. Jadoh is typically garnished with fresh cilantro and served with a side of spicy chutney made from ingredients like red chilies, garlic, and lime juice, adding an extra layer of flavor and heat. This dish is not only a delicious culinary treat but also holds cultural significance during the Wangala festival, symbolizing the unity and prosperity of the Garo community. Enjoying Jadoh with loved ones is a cherished tradition, strengthening bonds and fostering a sense of togetherness during this festive occasion.
