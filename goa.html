SHIGOMA FESTIVAL: BEBINCA

Shigmo is a lively Hindu festival celebrated in Goa, marking the arrival of spring and the harvest season. It features colorful processions, traditional folk dances like Ghode Modni and Fugdi, and vibrant parades, fostering a sense of community and joy. The festival lasts around two weeks and is a time for seeking blessings and celebrating abundance. Bebinca, a renowned Goan dessert with Portuguese influence, is a multi-layered pudding-like cake made from coconut milk, sugar, eggs, and flour. Each layer is individually baked, resulting in a rich and flavorful delicacy. Bebinca holds significance in Goan cuisine and is savored during festive occasions such as Christmas and Easter, symbolizing the region's culinary heritage and the spirit of togetherness. Both Shigmo and Bebinca represent the cultural richness and traditions of Goa, bringing people together in celebration and gratitude.
