YASOSHANG FESTIVAL:EROMBA DISH

Eromba is a traditional Manipuri dish commonly prepared during the Yaoshang festival, a vibrant five-day festival celebrated in Manipur. Yaoshang usually falls in March and marks the arrival of spring. Eromba is a savory and spicy dish made primarily from boiled vegetables, particularly a variety of greens like spinach, mustard greens, and various herbs. The vegetables are mashed together with fermented fish, typically ngari, which adds a distinct umami flavor to the dish.Other ingredients such as red chili paste, garlic, ginger, and sometimes bamboo shoots are added to enhance the flavor and aroma of Eromba.The dish is typically served with steamed rice, providing a hearty and nutritious meal during the festive celebrations of Yaoshang. Eromba holds cultural significance in Manipur, symbolizing unity and community as it is often prepared and shared among family and friends during the festival. Its flavorful and aromatic profile reflects the culinary heritage of Manipur and adds to the festive spirit of Yaoshang.
