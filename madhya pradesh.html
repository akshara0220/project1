KHAJURAHO DANCE FESTIVAL:POHA

Poha is a popular dish enjoyed during the Khajuraho Dance Festival, a vibrant cultural celebration held annually in Madhya Pradesh. The festival typically takes place in February or March and showcases classical dance forms from across India against the backdrop of the stunning Khajuraho temples. Poha, also known as flattened rice, is a light and nutritious dish made from flattened rice flakes that are dry-roasted or soaked and then stir-fried with various spices and ingredients. In Madhya Pradesh, Poha is often prepared with onions, green chilies, mustard seeds, curry leaves, turmeric, and sometimes potatoes or peanuts, adding texture and flavor to the dish. The result is a flavorful and aromatic delicacy that is both satisfying and easy to digest, making it an ideal choice for breakfast or as a snack during the Khajuraho Dance Festival. Poha reflects the culinary diversity of Madhya Pradesh and is a favorite among locals and visitors alike, offering a delightful culinary experience amidst the cultural extravaganza of the Khajuraho Dance Festival.
