UGADI FESTIVAL: BOBBATLU

Andhra Pradesh: Bobbatlu, also known as Puran Poli in some regions, is a traditional sweet snack that holds a special place in Andhra Pradesh cuisine, especially during festivals like Ugadi. Ugadi is the Telugu New Year, celebrated with great enthusiasm and fervor in Andhra Pradesh.Bobbatlu is essentially a sweet flatbread made by stuffing a mixture of chana dal (split chickpeas), jaggery (unrefined cane sugar), grated coconut, cardamom powder, and sometimes a hint of nutmeg or other spices, into a soft dough made from all-purpose flour or whole wheat flourr.The dough is then rolled out into thin circles, stuffed with the sweet filling, sealed, and cooked on a griddle or tawa with ghee (clarified butter) until golden brown and cooked through.During Ugadi, families come together to celebrate and enjoy traditional dishes like Bobbatlu, symbolizing the sweetness and auspiciousness of the occasion.
