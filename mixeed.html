ONAM FESTIVAL:SADYA

Sadya is the quintessential feast of the Onam festival in Kerala, celebrated with grandeur and fervor. This elaborate vegetarian meal is served on banana leaves, featuring an array of dishes meticulously arranged in a specific order. At the heart of Sadya is the fragrant rice, surrounded by an assortment of savory delights like curries, thoran, aviyal, olan, erissery, and sambar. Alongside these, pickles, pachadi, and papadum add bursts of flavor and texture. The grand finale of Sadya is the indulgent payasam, a traditional Kerala dessert made with rice, lentils, coconut milk, jaggery, and nuts. Sadya not only tantalizes the taste buds but also symbolizes unity, abundance, and the rich cultural heritage of Kerala.

ASSAM FOOD

BIHU FESTIVAL: PITA DISH

Pita is a traditional food prepared during the Bihu festival in Assam, particularly during Magh Bihu, also known as Bhogali Bihu, which is celebrated in January. Bihu is one of the most important festivals in Assam, celebrating the harvest season and marking the end of the harvesting period. Pita is essentially a type of rice cake or pancake made from a mixture of rice flour, jaggery (unrefined cane sugar), and sometimes grated coconut. The ingredients are mixed together to form a smooth batter, which is then poured onto a hot griddle or tawa and cooked until golden brown and crispy on the outside. Pita is typically enjoyed with various accompaniments, such as gur (date palm jaggery) or molasses, and sometimes with a sprinkle of sesame seeds for added flavor. Pita holds a special place in the culinary traditions of Assam and is an integral part of the Bihu festival, symbolizing abundance, prosperity, and togetherness.
