HERMIT FESTIVAL:ROGAN JOSH

The Hermit Festival, or Amarnath Yatra, in Jammu and Kashmir, is a revered pilgrimage to the Amarnath Cave, dedicated to Lord Shiva. It occurs during the Hindu month of Shravan (July-August), drawing devotees from across India for a challenging trek through the Himalayas. Pilgrims seek blessings from the naturally formed ice lingam inside the cave, symbolizing Lord Shiva. Rogan Josh, a staple of Kashmiri cuisine, is a flavorful dish featuring tender lamb or goat meat cooked in a rich sauce of Kashmiri spices. Its name translates to "red meat," reflecting its vibrant color from Kashmiri red chili powder. The dish boasts a complex blend of cloves, cinnamon, cardamom, and fennel seeds, slow-cooked to perfection. Prepared in copper pots, Rogan Josh is known for its tender meat and velvety gravy, complemented by aromatic spices. It is often served with steamed rice or naan, garnished with coriander leaves or a dollop of yogurt. Rogan Josh embodies the culinary heritage and cultural richness of Kashmir, offering a delightful sensory experience to those who savor it.
