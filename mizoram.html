SHAPCHUR FESTIVAL: KIT-BAMBOO SHOOT CURRY

During the Shapchur festival in Mizoram, Kit-Bamboo ShoOt Curry is a traditional delicacy that holds a special place. Shapchur is a harvest festival celebrated by the Mizo community in November, marking the end of the agricultural season.Kit-Bamboo, also known as short bamboo shoots, are a key ingredient in this dish. These bamboo shoots are harvested and cut into small pieces, then cooked with various spices and herbs to create a flavorful curry. The curry typically includes ingredients like pork, smoked meat, or fish along with bamboo shoots, imparting a rich and hearty flavor to the dish.The spices used in the curry may vary, but commonly include ginger, garlic, turmeric, and chili peppers, adding depth and heat to the dish.Kit-Bamboo Short Curry is often served with rice or traditional Mizo bread, providing a wholesome and satisfying meal during the Shapchur festivities.This dish is not only a culinary delight but also a symbol of the Mizo culture and heritage, reflecting the importance of bamboo and the harvest season in their lives.
